CAREER

Klay Thompson declared for the 2011 NBA draft after his junior year in college. That same year, Klay was competing against Kyrie Irving, Kemba Walker, Kawhi Leonard, and Isaiah Thomas. Eventually, he was selected by the Golden State Warriors in the 11th pick.

After not being selected to play in the All-Star Weekend, Klay improved drastically in all areas. Soon, he scored a season-high 26 points against the Boston Celtics. The next week, he beat his record with 27 points against the New Orleans Hornets. In March, he averaged 30 minutes per game, and was voted to the NBA All-Rookie team at the end of the season.

Klay Thompson is known for his spot-up shooting that is often described as “textbook” and “picture-perfect”. When his elite shooting is combined with Stephen Curry’s, they transform into the legendary Splash Brothers. In the 2014-15 season, they combined to sink 525 three-pointers, the most ever by an NBA duo. In February 2016, when the Warriors visited the White House, President Barack Obama said that Klay’s jump shot was “actually a little prettier” than Curry’s.
